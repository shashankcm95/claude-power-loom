# HT.3.2 — measurement-methodology.md reframe

## Context

Per post-HT audit-followup (5-agent chaos test + HETS code review) — two senior-architect agents independently flagged `swarm/measurement-methodology.md` as "institutional commitment dressed as observed practice." Convergent FLAG; cross-validates the institutional-framing concern.

The doc currently declares itself observation-only:

- Line 7: "**NOT an ADR**: captures observed practice, not new institutional invariant"
- Line 23: "Prescriptive rules — future phases reference observed practice; nothing here binds them"
- Line 25: "Institutional commitment — not an ADR; substrate ADR ledger stays at 4 (ADR-0001/0002/0003/0005)"

But the 5 canonical patterns use imperative voice that reads as institutional commitment:

- Line 108: "**Never cite a raw grep count without per-site classification.** Classify per-site:"
- Line 117: "**Name the measurement method when citing** — visual estimate, analytical claim, and fence-line empirical can yield different counts."
- Line 132: "**Count broken references against the actual filesystem at sub-plan time**, not against the audit's expected target list."
- Line 139: "**Before 'delete + migrate' decisions, grep ALL invocation forms**:"
- Line 152: "**When a decision-block enumerates N options, identify the underlying axes.** Collapsed axes lead to silent decision gaps."

Plus the Scope-axis disambiguation section at lines 184-191: "**When an audit says 'N candidates', clarify the scope axis**: [...] **Specify which scope when citing**."

The audit's reframing: imperative voice reads as binding rule; doc's self-declared positioning says it's not binding rule; the gap creates ambiguity for readers about whether the substrate is committing to these patterns or just documenting them. Two paths to close the gap:

1. **Strip imperative voice** — reframe patterns as descriptive observed-practice. Preserves doc's existing "not an ADR" positioning. Doc remains an editorial-tier observed-practice catalog.
2. **Promote to ADR** — recognize that imperative voice reflects genuine institutional commitment; codify as editorial-tier ADR per ADR-0004 taxonomy.

**HT.3.2 chooses path 1** (strip imperative voice) because:

- The doc's content IS observation (HT.1 + HT.2 dogfooded patterns documented retrospectively), not new invariant the substrate commits to
- The ADR ledger just grew 4 → 5 with HT.3.1 (ADR-0004); inflating to 6 with an editorial-tier observed-practice ADR contradicts the HT.0.9-verify FLAG-5 right-sizing pattern that has held substrate to 5 ADRs
- Promoting to ADR would require restating the 5 patterns as substrate invariants (e.g., "Substrate phases will classify all grep counts per-site"), which over-promises — future phases may or may not adopt these patterns
- Path 1 preserves the doc's existing institutional position; path 2 changes it

**Why now (HT.3.2 timing)**: Tier 2 institutional reframing second sub-phase; closes the framing-vs-content gap surfaced by external auditor. Sequenced after HT.3.1 (ADR-0004 establishes editorial-tier framing this doc cites) and before HT.3.3 (ADR-0002 status flip; orthogonal to measurement-methodology).

**Why sub-plan-only methodology** (per HT.1.6 decision-rationale-matrix convention):

| Trigger | Status | Reasoning |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Fresh design surface | NO | 5 patterns already authored at HT.2.1; HT.3.2 reframes voice, not content |

5 of 5 triggers absent. Net: sub-plan-only with explicit decision-rationale matrix per HT.2.4 pure-doc precedent.
