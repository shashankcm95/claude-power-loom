- **Date:** 2026-06-29
- **RFC:** [`2026-06-29-oq3-kernel-seal-lesson-provenance.md`](../rfcs/2026-06-29-oq3-kernel-seal-lesson-provenance.md) §5.4. **W3 of 3** — the wave that makes OQ-3 a *real* predecessor (a chain-resident, cross-uid-VERIFIABLE `(approval_hash, lesson_commitment)` binding), not a forgeable-field-adder.
- **Predecessors:** W1 (#459, the commitment + reorder), W2 (#460, the approval-layer binding — `verifyApproval` now returns the verified `body` carrying `{approvedAt, nonce, key_id, sig}`, the bundle source).

W3 persists the binding onto the chain; **PR-A2** (follow-on) verifies it.

## Goal

After W3 the chain **persists** a cross-uid-**verifiable** `(approval_hash, lesson_commitment)` binding — verifiable in the sense that the binding carries a public broker signature PR-A2 can crypto-verify. **The enforcement locus is PR-A2, not W3.** At rest the W3 stores **shape-validate** `broker_sig` (canonical-base64, 64-byte) — they hold no verify key and never call `verifyRecordSig`, by design — so a same-uid forger lacking the broker key CAN write a self-consistent record with a well-shaped-but-bogus sig; it is integrity-sealed (id-derive + content_hash), NOT provenance-checked, until **PR-A2 crypto-verifies** the sig over `approvalSigBasis({hash:approval_hash, approvedAt, nonce, key_id, lesson_commitment})` and rejects the bogus sig (RFC §5.5). And even then the unforgeability is **gated on the broker actually running cross-uid** — merged code NARROWS, deployment HARDENS (OQ-NS-6). W3's job is to put the broker-signed bundle on the chain so PR-A2 *can* verify it. Two halves (RFC §5.4):
1. **SEAL** `lesson_commitment` into the join-key id basis → an in-place tamper re-derives a mismatching id → rejected on read (tamper-evident).
2. **RECORD** the broker-sig provenance bundle `{approvedAt, nonce, key_id, broker_sig}` as recorded fields — **self-protecting**: `broker_sig` is over `approvalSigBasis({hash:approval_hash, approvedAt, nonce, key_id, lesson_commitment})` (the W2 basis), so a same-uid edit of any of them breaks the sig PR-A2 will verify. Then **propagate** through the merge-observer into the merge-outcome record.
