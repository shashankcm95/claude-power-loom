## Context

**Why now.** Wave 2 (the verification tier) opens by removing the cancelled-K8 debris so the new
R9/R11/R12 surface is built on a clean base. Two artifacts ship dead:

- **K6** (`packages/kernel/enforcement/k6-subset-check.js`, `checkSubset`/`isSubset`) — a pure
  capability set-subset engine. Shipped **dormant** in v3.1 PR-2a; its intended first consumer was
  **K8** (the tool-mask gate). K8 was **DROPPED** (ADR-0012: a PreToolUse hook's `updatedInput` is
  inert on Agent/Task spawns), so K6 never gained a production importer. The agent.md↔contract
  reconciliation validator does its own write/subprocess set-math inline — it does **not** call K6.
  Boundary decision #216: **retire** rather than carry a 4th-phase dormant primitive (YAGNI).
- **pre-spawn-tool-mask** (`packages/kernel/hooks/pre/pre-spawn-tool-mask.js`) — the K8-era tool-mask
  PreToolUse hook. **Unregistered + inert since it shipped** (ADR-0012: its network-strip never took
  effect). Documented as such in a `hooks.json` `_comment`.

**Scope boundary.** This retires the **K6 + its K8-era tool-mask twin** only. K3.b (`buildEnvelope`,
the *other* dormant primitive built for K8) is **separately deferred to v3.3 with its A6 consumer** —
NOT in scope (it keeps its own `dormancy-assertion-k3b` CI job).

## Principle Audit

- **YAGNI** — the core driver: K6 + tool-mask have been speculative across **three phases** (v3.1→v3.2) with zero consumer; their consumer (K8) is cancelled. Deleting beats carrying dead code "in case."
- **KISS** — removing the dormant twin shrinks the kernel surface the verification tier must reason about; fewer "is this live?" questions for R9/R11 authors.
- **DRY** — resolves the standing K6/validator DRY known-debt (ROADMAP:86) by *eliminating* one of the two write/subprocess-containment implementations rather than converging them (the reconciliation validator's inline set-math stays as the single live implementation).
- **SRP** — the retire PR has exactly one reason to change (remove cancelled-K8 debris); doc edits are the honest side-effects of that one reason, not scope creep.
- **OCP** — removal is subtractive; no live behavior extends or depends on the deleted surface (proven by the zero-importer probe).
