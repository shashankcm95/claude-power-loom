# RFC — OQ-3 kernel-seal: binding the captured lesson into the egress approval

- **Date:** 2026-06-29
- **Status:** DRAFT — proposed. The hard-predecessor the world-anchored-lesson HARDEN gate is blocked behind (USER decision 2026-06-29: land OQ-3 *first*, as its own RFC; the gate RFC is the follow-on).
- **Author:** OQ-3 design pass (5-lens egress recon + prior-RFC reconciliation).
- **Supersedes / extends:** resolves **OQ-3** of [`2026-06-18-authenticated-minter-provenance-close.md`](2026-06-18-authenticated-minter-provenance-close.md) ("move the gated weight's authoritative basis onto the kernel chain"). Predecessor of the parked first-weight-gate RFC (world-anchored lesson confidence to live recall). Tied to the autonomous-SDE capture wire (#447-#457), `OQ-NS-6`, the `#273` family, the `[[beta-internal-verification-mandate]]`.

## 1. Summary

The capture wire (#447-#457) mints a **world-anchored lesson**: a lesson bound to a gh-verified merge plus the kernel-sealed `approval_hash` (`world-anchor-mint.js:383` binds `to_delta_ref = record.approval_hash`). The parked weight-gate RFC proposed admitting that lesson's confidence as the first weight to **gate** a real decision (live persona recall). Its verify board found the load-bearing flaw: **the merge half is kernel-sealed, but the LESSON half is co-forgeable lab state.** `approval_hash` commits `{repo, issueRef, diff}` (`approval.js:46-63`) and says **nothing** about which lesson rode that merge. A same-uid writer co-forges a `live_pending` node (an attacker lesson) plus the attestation that resolves it, and the mint binds that attacker lesson to a genuine merge's sealed `approval_hash`. Per `OQ-NS-6` a HARDEN gate that promotes a co-forged input is unsafe, so the gate inherits the 2026-06-18 RFC's OQ-3 hard-predecessor.

This RFC closes OQ-3 for the lesson lane: **bind the captured lesson into the broker-signed egress approval at emit, and persist that broker-signed binding onto the kernel join-key** (the bare commitment alone would be same-uid co-forgeable; the verify board showed the cross-uid signature must survive forward or PR-A2 recomputes from a forgeable input and gains nothing). The result: a genuine merge's `approval_hash` is locked to the specific lesson that rode it by a cross-uid-**verifiable** signature, not merely a content-address. It is a kernel-egress change. It stays SHADOW and weight-inert (no consumer reads the binding for a weight; `LIVE_SOURCES` stays `Object.freeze([])`). It is the predecessor that puts the world-anchored HARDEN gate's authoritative basis — cross-uid-verifiable — on the kernel chain; it is **not** itself the gate, it does **not** prove lesson-correctness, and the verifiability is real only once the broker actually runs cross-uid (the standing `OQ-NS-6` deployment residual). The gate, the consumer flip, and the world-anchor minting are the deferred PR-A2 follow-on.

## 2. The hard-predecessor, precisely

The 2026-06-18 RFC's P2 entry sequence (its §"P2 entry sequence", step 2) requires: *move the gated weight's authoritative basis onto the kernel transaction chain.* For the world-anchored-lesson gate the authoritative basis is the **(merge, lesson) binding**. Its two halves today:

- **Merge half — kernel-sealed (have).** `approval_hash = computeEmissionHash({repo, issueRef, diff})` is the content-address of exactly the bytes that shipped under a valid broker-signed human approval (`approval.js:19-25`, `join-key-store.js` header). The merge-observer copies it from the kernel join-key into the merge-outcome record (`merge-observer.js:107`); the mint binds it (`world-anchor-mint.js:383`).
- **Lesson half — co-forgeable lab state (missing).** Nothing on the kernel chain commits the lesson. The lesson lives only in the `live_pending` node (`live-pending-store.js`, `@loom-layer: lab`, same-uid writable) and the world-anchored node (`live-recall-store.js`, same). The mint joins them by an **exact-set match** on `(repo-slug, issue_ref, lesson_signature)` (`world-anchor-mint.js:217-226`), which its own header calls out as **dedup/correctness, not authorization** (`world-anchor-mint.js:50-60`): for any uncontested `(repo, issue, sig)` a same-uid attacker co-forges one captured node plus one attestation, gets exactly-one match, and the mint world-anchors the attacker body with no refuse.

So the binding's authoritative basis is half kernel, half co-forgeable lab. OQ-3 = move the lesson half onto the kernel chain so a later authenticated writer (PR-A2) can recompute the whole binding from a kernel-resident source.

## 3. What exists today (and its honest limits)

- **`approval.js`** — `emissionAxiom`/`computeEmissionHash` (the emission content-address, `{repo, issueRef, diff}`); `approvalSigBasis({hash, approvedAt, nonce, key_id})` (the freshness-bound basis the broker signs); `verifyApproval` (re-derives the emission hash from the file's own `emission`, checks the broker sig over the basis, fail-closed). **Limit:** none of these commits the lesson.
- **`emit-pr.js`** — the sole `emitPR` chokepoint. `data` is untrusted `{repo, issueRef, diff}`; the approval gate keys on `computeEmissionHash(draft)`; on emit-success it seals the kernel join-key via `writeJoinKey` (`emit-pr.js:481-490`). **Limit:** `data` carries no lesson; the join-key id basis is `{repo, issueRef, pr_number, approval_hash}` (`join-key-store.js` header) with no lesson field.
- **`live-draft-run.js`** — the live solve loop. Order today: solve, grade, **emit** (`solveGradeDraftOne` `emitFn` at `:288`), write artifact, **capture** (`captureLiveLesson` at `:313`). `captureLiveLesson` derives the lesson and mints the `live_pending` node (`:181-226`). **Limit:** capture runs **after** emit, so the lesson does not exist when the approval is computed — even though its inputs (candidate, verdict) exist before emit.
- **The world-anchored node** (`live-recall-store.js:61`) carries `lesson_signature` + `lesson_body` (both also on the `live_pending` node). So a stable `(lesson_signature, lesson_body)` commitment is re-derivable on both the capture side and the gate side — the property the seal needs.
- **The cross-uid broker** (`loom-broker-sign.js`/`-bind.js`/`-launch.js`, verified by `approval.js verifyApproval`) — LIVE for egress approval. It signs the approval basis. **Limit:** it is hardwired to the egress approval context; and the same-uid residual (the broker is forgeable until it actually runs under a separate uid) is a deployment property, not closed by this RFC.

**Reading:** every surface needed for the seal already exists; the lesson is simply never threaded into the approval or the join-key, and capture runs a step too late.

## 4. The recon correction — NOT `computeEmissionHash`

The pre-RFC recon note said "extend `computeEmissionHash`." That is **wrong**, and stating why is load-bearing:

`computeEmissionHash` binds **what is emitted** — the scrubbed diff — and `approval.js:19-25` declares a **Forward-Contract**: ③.2.5 (`gh-emit.js`) re-derives `computeEmissionHash` over the **actual emission payload** and refuses on mismatch. The lesson is a lab artifact; it is **never emitted** in the PR. Putting `lesson_commitment` inside the emission axiom would make the emission hash depend on something not in the emission, breaking the re-derive-from-emitted-bytes check (gh-emit has no lesson to re-hash). The lesson belongs at the **approval layer** — what the human and broker approve **alongside** the emission — not the emission-identity layer. This keeps the emission Forward-Contract byte-identical.

## 5. Design — bind at the approval layer, persist the broker sig onto the kernel join-key

### 5.1 The lesson commitment (definition + the two invariants the build must honor)

`lesson_commitment = sha256(canonicalJsonSerialize({ lesson_signature, lesson_body }))` — a 64-hex digest over the lesson's taxonomy axis plus its distinguishing prose. Both fields are persisted by the capture-side `live_pending` node (`live-pending-store.js:71`, `STORED_KEYS`) **and** the gate-side world-anchored node (`live-recall-store.js:61`), so the consumer re-derives it from the verified node body at gate time. The diff is committed separately by `computeEmissionHash`; `candidate_patch_sha` is deliberately excluded — the world-anchored node drops it, so including it would make the commitment un-re-derivable gate-side (board OQ3-1: resolved to `(lesson_signature, lesson_body)`).

- **ALWAYS-A-STRING (the canonical-hash footgun, board hacker MEDIUM).** `canonicalJsonSerialize` emits the literal token `undefined` for an `undefined`-valued key (`canonical-json.js:53`), so `undefined`, `''`, and key-absent are **three distinct basis strings** — passing `undefined` into a basis makes the broker sign over a different string than the human approved. So `captureLiveLesson` returns `lesson_commitment` as a string on **every** branch: a 64-hex digest when a lesson was captured, `''` on every fail-soft branch (ineligible / off-floor / derive-threw). The emit + approve paths **coerce** a missing value to `''` and **reject** `undefined` explicitly before it enters any basis. A unit test asserts the `undefined`-input path produces the **same** basis as explicit `''`.
- **BYTE-IDENTICAL ROUND-TRIP (board hacker LOW).** The bytes `captureLiveLesson` commits to MUST equal the bytes the `live_pending` store persists and the mint seats into the world-anchored node verbatim — **no scrub/normalization between commitment and write.** This holds today (the captured lane writes `lesson_body` verbatim, `live-draft-run.js:215`; the mint consumes it verbatim, `world-anchor-mint.js:217-226`, `live-recall-store.js:127`). A test mints a node and asserts `sha256({sig,body})` of the stored node `===` the emit-time commitment, so a future lesson-normalization step cannot silently break the round-trip. NOTE the (acceptable) asymmetry: the commitment binds `lesson_body`, which is **outside** the `live_pending` `node_id` basis (excluded so a reword collides, `live-pending-store.js:37-38`) — the commitment binds **more** than the node id does, by design.

### 5.2 Capture-before-emit (the reorder; OQ3-4 cost hazard resolved)

`solveGradeDraftOne` reorders so `captureLiveLesson` runs **before** `emitFn`. The inputs already exist (candidate at `live-draft-run.js:265`, verdict at `:280-282`), so the move is mechanical; capture stays fail-soft. `captureLiveLesson` is extended to return `lesson_commitment`; the orchestrator threads it into the emit data: `emitFn({ repo, issueRef, diff, lesson_commitment }, {})`.

**OQ3-4 RESOLVED (board architect MEDIUM) — and scoped to ARMING, not W1 (recon finding).** The live deriver is a real `claude -p` spawn, and an unconditional reorder ahead of `emitPR`'s cap/etiquette short-circuit (`emit-pr.js:424-432`) would spend on records `emitPR` would refuse. The resolution: order it solve, grade, eligibility-gate, a **cheap cap/etiquette pre-check**, **capture (derive)**, emit — the deriver runs only when an emit is actually going to be attempted (stated sacrifice: a tiny TOCTOU window means at most a rare wasted derivation, bounded by the per-call cost cap; `emitPR`'s in-lock gate stays authoritative). **BUT** this matters only once the loop is **armed**: today `runLiveDraftLoop` calls `emitFn(data, {})` with **empty** custody opts (no `custodyCapStatePath` / `custodyEtiquetteLedgerPath`), so the cap/etiquette gate never fires there and there is nothing to pre-check. So **W1 just reorders + threads the commitment**; the cap/etiquette pre-check lands in the **arming wave** (when custody opts are wired into the loop, see §6 activation residual), not W1. The reorder is still required for the **human approval step** — the human approves the `(diff, lesson)` pair, so the lesson must exist when the approval is authored.

### 5.3 Approval-layer binding (the load-bearing tie; every surface named)

Prevents a **post-approval lesson swap**: because `lesson_commitment` rides as untrusted `data`, without an approval-layer check a same-uid process could hand `emitPR` a swapped commitment under a genuine `approval_hash`. The edits, by module (board architect HIGH: `approval-store.js` is the load-bearing module the first draft omitted):

- **`approval.js` (PURE).** Extend `approvalSigBasis` to `({hash, approvedAt, nonce, key_id, lesson_commitment})`; add a `requestedLessonCommitment` param to `verifyApproval`, require `body.lesson_commitment === requestedLessonCommitment` (mirroring `body.hash === requestedHash`, `approval.js:103`), and fold `body.lesson_commitment` into the re-derived sig basis. A new fail-closed reason token `lesson-commitment-mismatch` on the reason-bearing reject path (**observable** per `security.md`, never a silent `{ok:false}`).
- **`approval-store.js` (the fs/wiring + the broker sign/verify boundary).** `recordApproval` gains a `lesson_commitment` param, writes it into the persisted body and signs the **extended** basis (`approval-store.js:118-119,132`); `readVerifiedApproval` gains a `requestedLessonCommitment` param threaded into `verifyApproval` **and returns the verified body** (incl. `sig`, `approvedAt`, `nonce`, `key_id`) so `emit-pr` can persist the provenance bundle (§5.4); the mint-time boundary `verifyRecordSig` (`approval-store.js:128-131`) re-derives the extended basis. The approval file stays keyed by the emission hash alone — per-emission uniqueness is the emission hash; the lesson binding is enforced by the sig + the equality check.
- **`emit-pr.js`.** `data.lesson_commitment` is validated 64-hex-or-empty (a fresh shape gate); it is **not** a disposition key — emission-adjacent data like the diff — so it passes `assertDataIsPolicyFree` (`emit-pr.js:192-201`), and is threaded as `verifyApproval`'s `requestedLessonCommitment`.
- **`approve-cli.js`.** Records `lesson_commitment` in the approval file and has the broker sign the extended basis (`approve-cli.js:207-209`). OQ3-3: the human **sees the lesson body** at approval time (additive defense — they can reject a hazardous lesson), but the trust claim stays provenance-only.

The swap is closed (board hacker-probed against the real `signRecordId`/`verifyRecordSig`): approved commitment -> ok; swapped commitment -> `lesson-commitment-mismatch`; edited `body.lesson_commitment` with the old sig -> `sig-invalid`.

### 5.4 Persist the broker-sig onto the kernel join-key (the chain-resident, cross-uid-VERIFIABLE binding)

This is the step that makes OQ-3 a **real** predecessor, not a forgeable-field-adder. The verify board (hacker MEDIUM) found the broker signature — the only cross-uid provenance fact — is one-shot **unlinked** at emit (`consumeApproval`, `emit-pr.js:467`), so a bare `lesson_commitment` sealed into the join-key would be **integrity-only** (same-uid co-forgeable, since the content-address recipes are exported), and PR-A2 recomputing from it would gain no provenance (the parent RFC's named trap: "recompute from a co-forgeable lab store, no provenance gained"). So OQ-3 **persists the broker signature forward**:

- **SEAL the binding.** Add `lesson_commitment` to the join-key id basis `{repo, issueRef, pr_number, approval_hash}` to `{..., lesson_commitment}` (`deriveJoinKeyId`, `join-key-store.js:124-132`), so an in-place tamper re-derives a mismatching id. Enumerate the closed-shape/validation surface the read path enforces (board architect LOW): `CORE_KEYS` + `WITH_BUILT_BY_KEYS` (the exact-set), `validateRecord` (a 64-hex-or-empty gate), `bodiesEqual` (collision).
- **RECORD the provenance bundle.** Persist `{approvedAt, nonce, key_id, broker_sig}` as recorded fields. They are **self-protecting**: `broker_sig` is over `approvalSigBasis({approval_hash, approvedAt, nonce, key_id, lesson_commitment})`, so a same-uid edit of any of them breaks the sig verify (fail-closed). `emit-pr` has all of these in hand (`readVerifiedApproval` now returns the verified body, §5.3). The `nonce` is already consumed (no replay); the sig is public-verifiable (no secret leak).
- **PROPAGATE.** The merge-observer (the SOLE join-key reader; the dam `REQUIRE_ALLOWLIST` `{emit-pr.js, merge-observer.js}` is **unaffected** — adding a body field adds no reader) copies the bundle into the merge-outcome record alongside `approval_hash` (`merge-observer.js:107`); `merge-outcome-store.js` adds the fields to `OUTCOME_KEYS` (`:88-91`) + `validateRecord` + the `content_hash` basis (`:108-112`).

After §5.4 the chain carries, end to end, a cross-uid-**verifiable** `(approval_hash, lesson_commitment)` binding: a same-uid forger cannot mint a join-key/merge-outcome that binds an attacker lesson to a genuine `approval_hash` without a broker signature it cannot produce. (Still gated on the broker actually running cross-uid — the standing `OQ-NS-6` deployment residual; merged code narrows, deployment hardens.)

### 5.5 The PR-A2 contract + what is NOT done here

OQ-3 stops at the chain-resident, broker-signed binding. **PR-A2** (the follow-on), at world-anchor mint time, reads the merge-outcome's provenance bundle and:

1. **Verifies** `broker_sig` over `approvalSigBasis({approval_hash, approvedAt, nonce, key_id, lesson_commitment})` against the custody-pinned verify key (`allowEnvFallback:false`) — the cross-uid authentication. Fail-closed.
2. **Re-derives** `sha256(canonical({node.lesson_signature, node.lesson_body}))` from the **verified** world-anchored node and asserts it `===` the bundle's `lesson_commitment`. This is the load-bearing check (board architect MEDIUM): the mint's own join is by `lesson_signature` **alone** (`world-anchor-mint.js:217-226`), so the commitment (which binds the body) is strictly stronger and catches a `lesson_body` reword under a kept `lesson_signature`.
3. **Mints** the world-anchor weight signed with the cross-uid key; the gate verifies **that**.

OQ-3 changes **no** consumer, flips **no** `LIVE_SOURCES`, mints **no** weight — it is weight-inert; the mechanism freezes pre-live per the mandate. The same-uid co-forge of the lab **body** (an attacker can still write a self-consistent world-anchored node) is **not** closed here and is **not** closed by the commitment alone: the commitment proves the body is **unchanged** since the approval bound it (integrity), the broker sig proves a **legitimate approval** bound it (provenance); body-**correctness** is never proven (§6).

## 6. Threat model & residuals (honest)

- **Closes (within this RFC's scope):** (a) the **post-approval lesson swap** (§5.3 — `emitPR` refuses a `lesson_commitment` the human/broker did not approve); and (b), once §5.4 lands, a **same-uid forge of the `(approval_hash, lesson_commitment)` binding that lacks a broker signature** — the binding now carries a cross-uid-verifiable provenance fact onto the chain (the board's correction: a bare commitment would NOT have closed this).
- **Narrows, does not close (`OQ-NS-6`, #273):** the same-uid co-forge of a self-consistent lab **body** (a world-anchored node whose own commitment re-derives) — the gate-side re-derive verifies **integrity** (the body is unchanged since the approval bound it), **not** body-**authenticity**. And the whole provenance fact is only as strong as the broker actually running under a separate uid the host cannot `read()` — a deployment property verified out-of-band. Merged code narrows; a deployed cross-uid broker + accumulated real merges harden.
- **Provenance, not correctness (NAMED).** A world-anchored merge proves **diff-acceptance, not lesson-correctness** (`lesson.js:56-62`: the maintainer corrected `LESSON_2137`). The seal binds "this lesson rode this approved-and-merged diff," never "this lesson is right."
- **The static (grandfather) floor has NO commitment (NAMED, board hacker LOW).** A world-anchored node minted from the static seed (Branch A, scrubbed body) carries no emit-time commitment. The follow-on gate MUST define its behavior — refuse a no-commitment node (commitment-required) or admit it on the static floor's own human-vetted provenance (OQ3-5).
- **Forward-contract churn.** Adding `lesson_commitment` to the approval sig basis and the join-key id basis changes those content-addresses for **every** emission (incl. no-lesson ones, where it is `''`). Acceptable now — ③.2.5 has no frozen standing approvals (24h TTL); land before any standing-approval corpus exists.
- **`emissionAxiom` untouched.** Per §4, `computeEmissionHash` and the emission Forward-Contract are byte-identical after this RFC (board honesty + architect CONFIRM, traced to `gh-emit.js:439-441`); only `approvalSigBasis` + the join-key basis grow.
- **ACTIVATION residual — the seal is doubly-inert until the live loop is ARMED (recon finding, post-board).** Capture lives in `runLiveDraftLoop` (the dry-run draft loop), which calls `emitFn(data, {})` with **empty** custody opts — so it does a dry-run emit and writes **no** join-key (the join-key write is on the armed path, `emit-pr.js:474`, gated on `custodyJoinKeyDir` + a live token + killswitch-off). The spec-kitty#2137 live emit was a **one-off operator proof**, not through `runLiveDraftLoop`; the production scheduler + the wire that arms the loop with custody opts is **lifecycle gap-map item 8, unbuilt**. So W1-W3 build the seal MECHANISM SHADOW; it **activates** (reaches a real join-key under a real approval) only once the loop is armed — a SEPARATE prerequisite. This is consistent with the observe-first / mechanism-freeze-pre-live mandate (the seal is one of the mechanisms that must freeze before live), but a reader must not mistake "W3 merged" for "the seal is live." The arming wave is where OQ3-4's cap/etiquette pre-check (§5.2) lands.

## 7. Phasing (each wave SHADOW; the per-wave VERIFY/build/VALIDATE discipline applies)

1. **W1 — the commitment + the reorder (lab-only, inert).** Add the single-source `computeLessonCommitment({lesson_signature, lesson_body})` helper (§5.1); `captureLiveLesson` returns an always-string `lesson_commitment`; reorder `captureLiveLesson` **before** `emitFn` in `solveGradeDraftOne`; thread it into the emit data. **No cap/etiquette pre-check in W1** — the loop's `emitFn(data, {})` has no cap state to pre-check (§5.2, §6 activation residual); that lands at arming. `emitPR` ignores an unknown data field today (board CONFIRM: a non-policy key passes `assertDataIsPolicyFree` and is never read), so this is observable-but-inert.
2. **W2 — the approval-layer binding (kernel + the broker sign/verify).** `approval.js` + `approval-store.js` + `emit-pr.js` + `approve-cli.js` (§5.3). Highest-rigor wave. **Acceptance tests:** a 64-hex `lesson_commitment` in data is accepted; a non-hex / 65-hex one is shape-rejected; `lesson_commitment` is NOT added to `DISPOSITION_KEYS`; a swapped/edited commitment fail-closes with `lesson-commitment-mismatch` / `sig-invalid`; the `undefined`-input basis `===` the `''` basis.
3. **W3 — the join-key seal + the provenance bundle + propagation (kernel + lab).** §5.4. Read-back/dedup/update immutability tests per the workspace rule, on **both** `join-key-store` and `merge-outcome-store`.
4. **(Follow-on, not this RFC) — PR-A2 + the world-anchored HARDEN gate.** PR-A2 verifies the persisted broker sig + the commitment re-derive (§5.5); then the parked weight-gate RFC's Phase 3-4.
